# CPI-India-Inflation

# Insights drawn:
1. The highest Y-O-Y growth rate of inflation was recorded in 2022 at 6.91%.
2. The inflation was high during 2022 due to several factors, one of the major factors could be the onset Russia-Ukraine conflict.
3. May 2023 is the month with the highest inflation, followed by October 2022 as the second-highest.
4. December 2022 and February 2023 show the share decline in inflation.
5. Food holds the highest weightage in CPI calculation at 50% followed by Miscellaneous at 19%.
6. In the M-O-M analysis in the food category, spices with a weightage of 16.60% emerge as one of the primary contributors to inflation, followed by cereals, which account for 12.09% of the inflationary pressures.
7. During COVID-19, a noticeable yearly increase during the first wave (2020) and second wave (2021) was observed. This significant jump indicates pandemic substantial impact on healthcare costs.
